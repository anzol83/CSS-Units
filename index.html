Understanding CSS Units

1. Absolute Units

Absolute units are fixed and do not change based on the parent element or viewport size. They are great for printed materials or fixed designs.

  • px (Pixels): Commonly used for defining dimensions in digital screens.
  • cm (Centimeters): Suitable for physical dimensions in print media.
  • mm (Millimeters): A smaller unit often used for precise measurements.
  • in (Inches): One inch equals 2.54 cm.
  • pt (Points): 1 point equals 1/72 of an inch.
  • pc (Picas): One pica equals 12 points.

2. Relative Units

Relative units adjust based on the context, such as the size of the parent element or the viewport.

  • em: Relative to the font size of the parent element.
  • rem: Relative to the root element's font size.
  • %: Percentage relative to the parent element.
  • vw (Viewport Width): A percentage of the viewport's width.
  • vh (Viewport Height): A percentage of the viewport's height.
  • vmin/vmax: Minimum or maximum of `vw` and `vh`.
